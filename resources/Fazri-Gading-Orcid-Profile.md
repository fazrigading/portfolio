### Automated Oil Palm Fruit Counting using Deep Learning for Reliable Yield Estimation in Natural Background

- **Year:** 2026
- **Type:** preprint
- **DOI:** [10.2139/ssrn.6717508](https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6717508)
- **Abstract:**

Accurate fruit counting is vital for yield estimation and harvest planning in oil palm plantations. Manual methods remain labor-intensive and prone to error because of dense clustering, occlusion, and varying illumination in natural environments. This study evaluates oil palm fresh fruit bunches (FFBs) cap-tured in real field conditions in East Kalimantan, Indonesia. The annotated images encompass diverse maturity stages, viewing angles, and lighting vari-ability within complex natural backgrounds. We performed a comparative evaluation of three state-of-the-art deep learning architectures: YOLOv5, YOLOv8, and Mask R-CNN. In addition to conventional detection metrics like Precision, Recall, and mAP, counting accuracy was rigorously assessed using Mean Absolute Error (MAE) across various fruit density scenarios. Experimental results show that YOLOv5 provides the most robust perfor-mance, achieving a Precision of 0.963, Recall of 0.946, and an F1-score of 0.954. Notably, YOLOv5 yielded the lowest global counting error with an MAE of 4.96 fruits per image. YOLOv8 and Mask R-CNN showed compet-itive detection capabilities, yet they exhibited higher counting variability in dense clusters. These findings establish a reliable foundation for automated yield estimation and provide a scalable solution for precision agriculture in the oil palm industry.

### Ganoderma Detection on Oil Palm Stem using YOLO Approach

- **Year:** 2025
- **Type:** conference-paper
- **Conference:** 2nd Beyond Technology Summit on Informatics International Conference (BTSI2C)
- **Date:** December 18, 2025
- **DOI:** [10.1109/bts-i2c67944.2025.11399519](https://doi.org/10.1109/bts-i2c67944.2025.11399519)
- **Abstract:** 

Ganoderma is the primary pathogenic fungus infecting oil palm stems and poses a serious threat to the palm oil industry, as it has the potential to cause significant economic losses. Accurate and timely detection is crucial for effectively controlling its spread. This study investigates the application of deep learning-based object detection to enable automated, precise, and rapid identification of Ganoderma infections. Specifically, it evaluates and compares the detection performance of medium variants of three models: YOLOv8, YOLOv9, and YOLOv10, each fine-tuned using pre-trained weights on a custom dataset of 354 annotated images containing instances of Ganoderma and Primordium (the early fruiting body). Through image augmentation, the dataset was expanded to 798 training images, 70 validation images, and 18 test images. Experimental results on the test set show that each model achieved mAP50 scores of 95.41%, 94.54%, and 95.10%, mAP50:95 scores of 74.40%, 69.74%, and 67.71%, and Mean Recall values of 93.93%, 92.91%, and 91.21%, respectively. These findings demonstrate that YOLOv8m strikes the optimal balance between detection accuracy and robustness, making it a strong candidate for deployment in early detection systems for oil palm plantations.
